## Company Overview

ResMed Inc. is a global medical device company headquartered in San Diego, CA (dual-listed on NYSE and ASX). Founded in 1989, the company designs, manufactures, and distributes cloud-connected medical devices and software for the treatment of sleep apnea, COPD, and other respiratory conditions.

**Business model:** Hardware (CPAP/BiPAP devices) + recurring consumables (masks/accessories replaced every 3-6 months) + SaaS (residential care software for DME providers and out-of-hospital care settings). Every device shipped since ~2014 has a cellular connectivity chip, creating a massive cloud-connected installed base.

**Key platforms:**
- **AirView:** Cloud-based clinician platform — 30M+ patients in ecosystem
- **myAir:** Consumer-facing patient engagement app — 9.3M+ downloads
- **Residential Care Software:** SaaS for DME (durable medical equipment) providers — ~12% of revenue
- **Cloud data:** ~21 billion nights of respiratory medical data stored

**CEO:** Michael Farrell (since 2013)
**Employees:** ~10,000+

## WHOOP Comparability Notes

### Why ResMed is in the Bucket 3 Comp Set

1. **Connected device + cloud platform model.** ResMed ships hardware with embedded cellular connectivity, generating continuous cloud data streams — structurally identical to WHOOP's wearable + subscription + cloud architecture
2. **Recurring revenue from hardware ecosystem.** Masks/accessories (36% of revenue) create a razor/razorblade dynamic similar to WHOOP's device + subscription model
3. **Software/SaaS layer on top of devices.** ResMed's 12% SaaS revenue from Brightree/MEDIFOX DAN is analogous to WHOOP's data analytics and coaching features
4. **FDA-cleared connected devices.** Both companies operate at the intersection of consumer tech and clinical health
5. **Data flywheel.** 21 billion nights of cloud data creates a competitive moat, analogous to WHOOP's aspirational health data platform thesis

### Key Differences (Why ResMed is Not a Perfect Comp)

| Dimension | ResMed | WHOOP |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue scale** | $5.1B | ~$1.1B run-rate |
| **Growth rate** | ~10% | ~103% (subscription) |
| **Market** | Prescription medical device (sleep apnea/COPD) | Consumer wellness wearable |
| **Regulatory moat** | Deep — FDA Class II, reimbursement-driven | Early — FDA ECG clearance, no reimbursement yet |
| **Customer** | Prescribed by physicians, paid by insurers | Direct-to-consumer subscription |
| **Revenue model** | Hardware sale + consumables replacement + SaaS | Subsidized hardware + subscription |
| **Gross margin** | 59% (blended device + consumables + software) | Likely 65-75% (subscription-heavy mix) |
| **Profitability** | Highly profitable (33% operating margin) | Recently cash flow positive |
| **Competition** | Oligopoly (Philips recalled, few alternatives) | Crowded (Apple Watch, Garmin, Oura, Fitbit) |

### Implied Valuation Read-Through for WHOOP

- ResMed trades at **6.0x EV/Revenue** as a mature, profitable, 10%-growth medical device + software company
- WHOOP at 103% growth deserves a significant growth premium over ResMed, but ResMed's profitability and regulatory moat justify a narrower discount than raw growth differentials might suggest
- The 6x revenue multiple anchors the **low end of Bucket 3** (health data / medical device range of 6-12x); iRhythm and Dexcom will anchor the higher-growth, higher-multiple end
- ResMed's Rule of 40 score of ~47 (via profitability) vs. WHOOP's estimated ~103+ (via growth) supports a premium for WHOOP, but the quality of ResMed's earnings is higher given its regulatory moat and insurance reimbursement
- ResMed's software/SaaS at 12% of revenue (and growing) validates the "device company becoming a platform company" narrative that is central to WHOOP's bull case
