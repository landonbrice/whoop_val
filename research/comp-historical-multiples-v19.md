# Historical Trading Multiples — Time Variance Analysis (V19)

**Date compiled:** April 16, 2026
**Assumption ID:** V19 — "Multiple time variance: today, 6mo, 12mo, 3yr"
**Purpose:** Track how EV/Revenue multiples for three key comps have shifted over time, and whether the Series G pricing anchored to stale multiples.

---

## Purpose

The $10.1B Series G mark implies ~9.2x EV/Revenue on WHOOP's $1.1B run-rate. To assess whether this is justified by current market conditions or anchored to historical multiples that have since compressed, we track three key comps across four time points:

1. **Garmin (GRMN)** — Bucket 1 hardware anchor
2. **Peloton (PTON)** — Bucket 2 cautionary comp
3. **Dexcom (DXCM)** — Bucket 3 aspirational anchor

The analytical question: **Did Bucket 3 multiples compress dramatically between the Series G negotiation period (late 2025 / early 2026) and the broader 2023-2024 reference frame investors likely used?**

### Price Reconstruction Notes

- **April 2023:** Garmin hit its 52-week low of ~$93.52 on March 13, 2023, recovering to ~$100-105 by mid-April. Market cap end of 2023 was $24.59B, suggesting mid-April was well below that at ~$19-20B. TTM revenue through Q1 2023 was approximately $4.86B (FY2022 $4.86B). EV/Revenue = ~$17.1B / $4.9B = ~3.5x.
- **April 2025:** Year-end 2024 market cap was $39.61B and year-end 2025 was $39.02B. April 2025 was likely in the $38-40B range based on the stock being ~$200. TTM revenue through Q1 2025 was ~$6.5B (FY2024 was $6.30B, Q1 2025 seasonally lighter). EV/Revenue = ~$36B / $6.5B = ~5.5x.
- **October 2025:** GRMN hit its then-ATH of $261.69 on October 9, 2025, with the monthly average ~$240-250. Market cap was ~$46B per macrotrends. TTM revenue through Q3 2025 was ~$6.7B. EV/Revenue = ~$43.6B / $6.7B = ~6.5x.

### Context

- **April 2023:** Post-COVID hangover. Fitness segment had declined 28% in FY2022. Market treating Garmin as a cyclical hardware company with limited growth. Multiple at historical median (~3.5-3.8x, consistent with GuruFocus 10-year median of 3.41-3.79x).
- **October 2025:** Garmin had delivered 3 consecutive years of strong Fitness segment growth (+21%, +32%, +33%). Record revenues. Market re-rated Garmin as a growth story, pushing multiple to the high end of its 10-year range (max 6.6x per GuruFocus). Near ATH.
- **April 2026:** Continued re-rating. At 6.7x, Garmin is at the top of its historical EV/Revenue range. This is NOT a normal multiple for Garmin — it reflects a cyclical high point.

### Implication for WHOOP

Garmin's multiple expansion from 3.5x to 6.7x was earned by accelerating Fitness segment growth. This represents the best-case scenario for a hardware company. WHOOP should be priced at a **premium** to Garmin (subscription model, higher gross margins), but using Garmin's current cyclical-peak multiple as the B1 anchor may overstate the stable-state valuation. A mid-cycle Garmin multiple of 4-5x is more conservative.

### Price Reconstruction Notes

- **April 2023:** Peloton was in the midst of its post-crash decline. Stock had fallen from $171 peak (Jan 2021) to the $8-14 range in early 2023. Year-end 2023 market cap was $2.19B, but mid-April 2023 the stock was still in the $10-12 range (before continued decline). Peloton had ~340M shares outstanding at the time. TTM revenue through March 2023 was approximately $2.9-3.0B (between FY2023's $2.80B and FY2022's $3.58B). Net debt was ~$700M (debt ~$1.7B less cash ~$1.0B). EV = ~$4.0-4.4B / $2.95B = ~1.3-1.5x.
- **April 2025:** Peloton stock was in the $5-6 range. Year-end 2024 market cap was $3.32B but that was after an August-November rally; by April 2025 the stock had pulled back. TTM revenue was ~$2.6B (FY2025 ended June 2025 at $2.49B, so Q3 FY2025 TTM was ~$2.55-2.60B). Net debt was declining (~$0.8B). EV = ~$2.8B / $2.6B = ~1.1x.
- **October 2025:** Per search results, Peloton stock was ~$7.50 in late October 2025, with market cap ~$3.0-3.2B. The Q1 FY2026 earnings (quarter ended Sept 2025) showed revenue of $551M and raised EBITDA guidance. TTM revenue was ~$2.49B. Net debt had declined to ~$0.5B. EV = ~$3.5B / $2.49B = ~1.4x.

### Context

- **April 2023:** CEO Barry McCarthy (replaced John Foley in Feb 2022) was executing turnaround. Revenue still declining YoY. Massive cash burn. Market priced Peloton as a distressed turnaround story. Multiple of ~1.3-1.5x reflected hope of stabilization but significant execution risk.
- **April 2025:** Revenue decline continued (-8% FY2025). All-time low of $2.70 hit in May 2024. Stock bounced from lows but investors skeptical of sustained turnaround. Multiple compressed to ~1.1x on continued revenue shrinkage.
- **October 2025:** Post-Q4 FY2025 rally. Peloton turned adjusted EBITDA positive and showed path to FCF breakeven. Stock rallied from ~$3 lows to ~$7.50. Multiple expanded briefly to ~1.4x on turnaround optimism.
- **April 2026:** Rally faded. Revenue still declining. CFO departure announced. Stock back to $4.77. Multiple at 1.2x — the market's steady-state assessment of a structurally challenged business.

### Implication for WHOOP

Peloton's multiple has been range-bound at 0.7-1.5x for three years despite significant operational changes. The market has essentially concluded that Peloton's business model (hardware + subscription with declining subscribers) deserves ~1x revenue. **This is the floor for WHOOP** — the "what if growth stops" scenario. The critical WHOOP bull argument is that WHOOP's 103% growth, higher subscription gross margins, and no hardware subsidization make Peloton an irrelevant comp. But Peloton at 1.2x is the gravitational pull if WHOOP's growth decelerates sharply.

### Price Reconstruction Notes

- **April 2023:** Year-end 2023 market cap was $47.95B, and the stock price peaked above $160 in late 2023 (November 2021 ATH was $162.81). In April 2023, DXCM was trading around $115-125 — this is pre-the-big-run-up that took it to $140+ by Q3 2023. With ~390M shares, market cap was ~$45-48B. TTM revenue through Q1 2023 was approximately $3.2-3.4B (FY2022 was $2.91B, growing rapidly). Dexcom had ~$2.5B cash and ~$1.3B debt, net cash ~$1.2B. EV = ~$45.6B / $3.3B = ~13.8x. **This is the critical data point.**
- **April 2025:** Dexcom was in the post-guidance-cut doldrums. The stock crashed ~40% in July 2024 (from ~$110 to ~$64) after the company slashed FY2024 guidance. By April 2025, the stock was still trading around $63 — essentially flat since the crash. Market cap ~$24.6B. TTM revenue through Q1 2025 was ~$4.1B. EV/Revenue = ~$23.9B / $4.1B = ~5.8x.
- **October 2025:** Stock had partially recovered to ~$68 range. Market cap was ~$21.5-26.5B (macrotrends shows $21.5B for Oct 30; trading economics showed $68.20 on Oct 30). Revenue was growing again. TTM through Q3 2025 was ~$4.4B. EV/Revenue = ~$26B / $4.4B = ~5.9x.

### Context — The Dexcom Multiple Compression Story

This is the most important analytical thread for the WHOOP valuation.

**Phase 1 — Peak (2021-2023): 10-14x EV/Revenue**
- Dexcom was the poster child for "health data platform" investing
- CGM market expanding rapidly; Dexcom held ~74% US share
- Revenue growing 20-28% annually
- Market priced in massive TAM expansion (Type 2 diabetes, general wellness)
- P/S ratio at end of 2023 was reportedly ~12-14x

**Phase 2 — Crash (July 2024): From ~10x to ~5x overnight**
- July 25-26, 2024: Stock crashed 40%+ in a single session
- Dexcom slashed FY2024 guidance from $4.20-4.35B to $4.00-4.05B
- CEO attributed miss to (1) salesforce restructuring, (2) lower per-customer revenue from rebates, (3) market share loss in DME channel
- JPMorgan downgraded from Overweight to Neutral, cut PT from $145 to $75
- This was Dexcom's worst trading day since IPO in 2005
- The crash destroyed ~$17B in market cap in two days

**Phase 3 — New Normal (2025-2026): 5-6x EV/Revenue**
- Revenue recovered (FY2025 $4.66B, +16% YoY) but the multiple never recovered
- Market re-priced Dexcom as a "good medical device company" rather than a "health data platform"
- The aspirational story (massive TAM, general wellness, OTC Stelo) lost credibility
- Current 5.0x multiple is essentially a mature medical device multiple — same neighborhood as ResMed (6.0x) and Masimo (6.4x)

### Implication for WHOOP

**This is the single most load-bearing finding for the WHOOP IC memo.** If Series G investors were anchoring WHOOP's Bucket 3 multiple to Dexcom's 2023-era 12-14x multiple, they were pricing off a multiple that has since compressed by 60%. Even if they were using early-2024 Dexcom multiples (~8-10x), the current reality is 5.0x.

The math is stark:
- At 2023 Dexcom multiples (12x), a 70% B3 weight on WHOOP = $10.5B blended EV. The Series G mark works.
- At current Dexcom multiples (5x), a 70% B3 weight on WHOOP = $5.4B blended EV. The Series G mark requires a ~90% growth premium.

## Analytical Implications for WHOOP Valuation

### 1. The Stale-Multiple Hypothesis

**Finding: Partially supported but not the full explanation.**

The Series G was priced in Q4 2025 / Q1 2026. By October 2025, Dexcom was already trading at ~5.9x — the crash had already happened 15 months prior. Sophisticated investors (IVP, Macquarie, QIA) would have been aware of Dexcom's compressed multiple. Therefore, the Series G pricing was NOT naively anchored to 2023 multiples.

However, the negotiation and due diligence likely started in mid-2025, when the memory of Dexcom at 10x+ was still fresh. Investors may have argued that Dexcom's compression was execution-specific (salesforce restructuring) rather than structural, and that the "health data platform" thesis remained intact for WHOOP.

### 2. The Three-Year Multiple Trajectory

| Metric | Apr 2023 | Apr 2025 | Oct 2025 | Apr 2026 | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| B1 (Garmin) | ~3.8x | ~5.5x | ~6.5x | 6.7x | Expanding (earned by growth) |
| B2 (Peloton) | ~1.5x | ~1.1x | ~1.4x | 1.2x | Range-bound (~1-1.5x) |
| B3 (Dexcom) | ~13.8x | ~5.8x | ~5.9x | 5.0x | Collapsed (structural re-rating) |

The trend is clear: Bucket 3 — the bucket that dominates WHOOP's bull case — has undergone a structural de-rating. The "health data platform" premium that once justified 10-14x multiples for medical device companies has evaporated.

### 3. What This Means for Bucket Weighting

Using **April 2023 multiples** with the Series G implied bucket weights (5/25/70):
- Blended = (0.05 x 3.8) + (0.25 x 1.5) + (0.70 x 13.8) = 0.19 + 0.38 + 9.66 = **10.2x** → Implies ~$11.2B. **The Series G mark of $10.1B is fully supported at 2023 multiples.**

Using **April 2026 multiples** with the same weights (5/25/70):
- Blended = (0.05 x 6.7) + (0.25 x 1.2) + (0.70 x 5.0) = 0.34 + 0.30 + 3.50 = **4.1x** → Implies ~$4.5B. **The Series G mark requires a ~125% premium to current comps.**

Using **October 2025 multiples** (when Series G was being negotiated) with same weights:
- Blended = (0.05 x 6.5) + (0.25 x 1.4) + (0.70 x 5.9) = 0.33 + 0.35 + 4.13 = **4.8x** → Implies ~$5.3B. **Still requires a ~90% premium.**

### 4. Reconciliation: How Does $10.1B Work?

The $10.1B mark at 9.2x EV/Revenue cannot be explained by static comp multiples at any recent time point except April 2023. The mark requires one or more of:

| Bridge Factor | Contribution | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Growth premium** (103% vs. comp avg 10-16%) | +3-4x over static comps | High — growth premium is real |
| **Real-option value** (healthcare platform) | +$2-4B additive | Medium — thesis credible but unproven |
| **Private market premium** / investor FOMO | +1-2x over public comps | Medium — strategic investors (Abbott, Mayo, QIA) pay access premium |
| **IPO option value** (18-24 month exit) | Embedded in price | High — investors buying at expected 20-30% discount to IPO |

### 5. Key Takeaway for IC Memo

**The Dexcom multiple compression story should be front-and-center in the memo.** It demonstrates that:

1. "Health data platform" multiples are not structural — they are cyclical and can compress 60% in 18 months
2. The comp set that would have justified $10.1B in 2023 no longer exists in public markets
3. WHOOP's valuation at 9.2x is a bet on the company's growth rate and healthcare optionality — it cannot be justified by current comparable company multiples alone
4. If WHOOP's growth decelerates to 20-30% (normal for a maturing subscription business), and Bucket 3 multiples remain at 5-6x, the implied valuation is $5-7B — a 30-50% haircut from the Series G mark

**Garmin's expansion provides partial offset:** Garmin's move from 3.5x to 6.7x shows that hardware/wearable multiples have expanded, but this is primarily driven by Garmin-specific Fitness segment growth and doesn't create a general-purpose multiple floor for the sector.
