# Precedent Transactions Analysis — WHOOP Valuation

## Date: April 16, 2026

## Purpose & Methodology

This analysis surveys M&A transactions in wearables, digital health, consumer subscription/fitness, and medical devices from 2020-2026 to establish a precedent transaction-implied valuation range for WHOOP. Precedent transactions are one of five triangulation methods (alongside intrinsic DCF, trading comps, last-round implied, and implied IPO range). Their role is a narrow-band reality check — not a primary valuation driver.

**Key methodological notes:**
- Deal inclusion window: 2020-2026, with 2023-2026 as the primary focus period
- Deal size threshold: $500M+ EV preferred; smaller deals included where strategically relevant
- Each deal tagged by market regime: ZIRP-peak (2020-2021), transition (2022-2023), current (2024-2026)
- Control premium is embedded in transaction multiples — no additional premium applied
- Deals where strategic synergies drove multiples 2x+ above peer median are flagged
- EV/LTM Revenue is the primary multiple; EV/NTM Revenue included where available
- All figures in USD unless noted

**WHOOP reference point:** $10.1B post-money (Series G, March 2026) on ~$1.1B revenue run-rate = ~9.2x implied EV/Revenue

## ZIRP vs. Current Regime Analysis

The regime shift from ZIRP-peak to current is the single most important contextual factor for interpreting precedent transactions.

### Consumer hardware / fitness: Massive destruction
- Mirror: $500M acquisition (2020) to $442M write-off (2022) = ~88% impairment
- Masimo/Sound United: $1.03B purchase (2022) to $350M sale (2025) = 66% loss
- Tonal: $1.9B valuation (2021) to ~$200-500M (2023) = 74-89% haircut
- Amazon Halo: Launched 2020, fully shut down July 2023

**Pattern:** Consumer hardware/fitness investments made during ZIRP have been destroyed almost universally. The exceptions are companies with strong subscription economics (Oura, Strava) or massive scale (Garmin, Apple Watch).

### Healthcare/medtech: Compressed but resilient
- Danaher/Masimo at 6.6x (current) vs. Microsoft/Nuance at 13.3x (transition)
- Current-regime medtech deals (Axonics 9.0x, Inari 8.1x) cluster around 8-9x
- CVS/Signify at 8-10x (transition) was close to where current-regime deals land

**Pattern:** Healthcare multiples have compressed ~30-40% from peak but stabilized in the 7-10x range for growing companies. Proprietary technology with FDA clearance still commands significant premiums.

### Implication for WHOOP
WHOOP's Series G at ~9.2x revenue sits right at the boundary between current-regime medtech transactions (8-9x) and above current-regime consumer subscription transactions (2-5x). The multiple is defensible ONLY if the market prices WHOOP primarily as a health-data platform (Bucket 3 weighting of 50%+). If WHOOP is primarily a consumer wearable (Bucket 1/2), the transaction evidence suggests 2-5x is the appropriate range.

## Implied WHOOP Range from Precedent Transactions

### Scenario-Based Implied Valuation

Using the three-bucket framework with transaction-derived multiples applied to WHOOP's ~$1.1B revenue run-rate:

| Scenario | B1 Weight / Multiple | B2 Weight / Multiple | B3 Weight / Multiple | Blended Multiple | Implied EV |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bear** (40/40/20) | 40% / 1.5x | 40% / 3.0x | 20% / 8.0x | **3.4x** | **$3.7B** |
| **Base** (20/40/40) | 20% / 1.5x | 40% / 3.5x | 40% / 8.5x | **5.1x** | **$5.6B** |
| **Bull** (10/30/60) | 10% / 1.5x | 30% / 4.0x | 60% / 9.0x | **6.8x** | **$7.4B** |
| **Series G implied** (5/25/70) | 5% / 1.5x | 25% / 4.0x | 70% / 9.5x | **7.7x** | **$8.5B** |

**Key observations:**
1. The bear case ($3.7B) implies WHOOP is worth ~63% less than its Series G mark — a significant gap
2. The base case ($5.6B) implies ~45% downside from the Series G — still a meaningful discount
3. The bull case ($7.4B) implies ~27% downside — closer but still below the mark
4. Even the most generous bucket weighting ($8.5B) implies ~16% downside from $10.1B

**This means precedent transactions alone do NOT support the $10.1B valuation.** The gap must be closed by:
- Real-option upside (healthcare reimbursement, B2B, international — NOT baked into these multiples)
- WHOOP's growth premium (103% vs. median comp growth of ~20-30%)
- The IPO exit pathway premium (Series G investors pricing in 12-18 month liquidity event)
- The control-premium discount (these are M&A transactions; WHOOP's Series G is a minority investment with no control premium embedded)

### Growth-Adjusted Analysis

Precedent transactions overwhelmingly involve companies growing at 10-35% (Masimo 7-10%, Inari 22%, Axonics 34%, Shockwave 49%). WHOOP at 103% growth is a significant outlier. Applying a crude growth-premium adjustment:

- Median current-regime medtech transaction: ~8.5x at ~25% growth
- Growth premium factor (Rule of 40 basis): WHOOP at 103% growth / 25% median = ~4.1x premium on growth
- But medtech multiples don't scale linearly with growth; a more conservative 1.5-2.0x growth premium is appropriate
- Growth-adjusted range: 8.5x * 1.5-2.0 = **12.8x - 17.0x** on the health-data portion
- Blended with consumer segments: **7-10x overall** seems achievable with growth premium

This growth-adjusted view brings the precedent-transaction implied range closer to the $10.1B mark, but only under bull-case assumptions about health-data weighting AND sustainability of 100%+ growth.

## Failed Deals & Market Signals

### Amazon Halo Shutdown (April 2023)

Amazon discontinued its Halo wearable device line effective July 31, 2023, offering full refunds. The Halo Band (launched 2020), Halo View, and Halo Rise were all terminated.

**Signal:** Even Amazon, with unlimited resources and distribution, could not build a viable wearable health business from scratch. The "increasingly crowded segment" rationale cited by Amazon validates that scale and distribution alone are insufficient — you need the subscription flywheel and community that WHOOP has built.

**WHOOP read:** Actually bullish for WHOOP. Amazon's exit removes a well-resourced competitor and confirms that the wearable health market rewards differentiated products with engaged user communities, not just hardware distribution.

### Teladoc / Livongo Write-Down ($20B+ impairment, 2022)

Total goodwill impairment of approximately $20B — exceeding the entire deal value. Teladoc's market cap as of early 2026 is ~$2-3B.

**Signal:** Peak-ZIRP digital health multiples (64x revenue) were entirely divorced from fundamentals. Even the best digital health assets of the era (Livongo was genuinely innovative) were dramatically overvalued. This is the cautionary anchor for any WHOOP valuation that relies heavily on "health platform" optionality.

### Mirror Write-Off ($442.7M impairment, 2022)

Lululemon wrote off 89% of the $500M Mirror acquisition value.

**Signal:** Connected fitness demand was a COVID anomaly, not a structural shift. Hardware + subscription models in fitness are extremely vulnerable to consumer preference shifts and competitive commoditization.

### Masimo / Sound United Value Destruction ($680M loss, 2022-2025)

Bought for $1.03B, sold for $350M three years later.

**Signal:** The "embed health sensors in consumer electronics" thesis does not work without genuine consumer demand for health data. Having the technology is necessary but not sufficient — you need the use case and the engagement model.

### Tonal Cram-Down (2023)

75-90% valuation haircut from $1.9B peak, after failing to find a buyer including Peloton.

**Signal:** Connected fitness hardware without a viable subscription flywheel becomes distressed quickly. The market for acquiring these assets is thin — even at steep discounts, buyers were scarce.

## Key Takeaways for WHOOP Valuation

1. **Precedent transactions imply a base-case range of $5-8B for WHOOP** — below the $10.1B Series G mark. The gap is real and must be bridged by growth premium, real-option value, and/or IPO liquidity premium.

2. **The regime matters enormously.** ZIRP-era transactions are essentially uninformative except as cautionary tales. Current-regime (2024-2026) medtech deals clustering at 7-10x are the most relevant anchors.

3. **Consumer hardware/fitness transactions have been universally value-destructive since 2021.** Every major connected-fitness M&A deal from the COVID era (Mirror, Sound United, Tonal) has been impaired 65-90%. WHOOP's valuation must explicitly explain why it is different.

4. **Healthcare/medtech transactions have been resilient at 7-10x.** Danaher/Masimo (6.6x), Stryker/Inari (8.1x), BSX/Axonics (9.0x), and CVS/Signify (8.6-10.4x) all validate the premium for health-data/device companies.

5. **WHOOP's 103% growth rate is a genuine differentiator** relative to the 7-30% growth rates in the precedent transaction set. Growth-adjusted, the $10.1B mark is achievable but requires health-data thesis credibility.

6. **The Peloton cautionary is alive in transaction data.** Mirror (wrote off), Precor (failed to sell), Tonal (cram-down) — every company that looked like "Peloton but for X" has been destroyed. WHOOP must pass the Peloton test.

7. **Control premium note:** Precedent transactions embed a 20-40% control premium that WHOOP's Series G does NOT have (it's a minority investment). Adjusting for this, the transaction-implied range on a minority basis would be approximately 20-30% lower, i.e., $4-6B — which makes the gap with the $10.1B mark even wider.
