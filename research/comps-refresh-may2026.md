### Analytical Implications for WHOOP

**1. The "peak-to-trough" defense is weaker now**
In the April analysis, we argued that Dexcom's ~13.8x multiple in 2023 could justify $10.1B if Series G investors used peak multiples. But now ALL health-data comps have compressed further. The "investors were using stale multiples" argument actually becomes STRONGER — the gap between peak and current has widened.

**2. Spotify provides the positive template**
Spotify went from 1.9x (unprofitable) → 6.0x (profitable) → 4.1x (market correction). If WHOOP can demonstrate profitability and sustained growth through IPO, a similar re-rating from ~4x to ~6x on recognized revenue ($650M × 6x = $3.9B) or on bookings ($1.1B × 6x = $6.6B) is plausible. Still doesn't reach $10.1B without a growth premium, but narrows the gap.

**3. Multiple compression is accelerating**
The 3-week compression (Apr 16 → May 5) is more severe than the prior 3-month drift. This may reflect macro factors (tariff uncertainty, rate environment) rather than company-specific issues. If markets recover, the comp multiples recover with them.
