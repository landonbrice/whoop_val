# Stress Test: WHOOP 60% International Revenue Claim

**Date:** April 16, 2026
**Purpose:** Verify or refute the claim that "~60% of WHOOP's sales come from outside the US"
**Status:** Complete

## 1. Source Tracing

### Original Source
The claim traces to a **New York Times DealBook article** by Niko Gallogly, published March 31, 2026, covering the Series G announcement.

- **Techmeme headline:** "Wearable maker Whoop raised $575M led by Collaborative Fund at a $10.1B valuation and says it hit $1B in ARR by 2025's end; 60% of its 2025 sales were non-US"
- **Original NYT URL:** https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/31/business/dealbook/whoop-a-wearable-health-device-maker-raises-575-million.html (paywalled)
- **Author:** Niko Gallogly, NYT DealBook reporter
- **Techmeme source:** https://www.techmeme.com/260331/p6

### Key Distinction: "60% of NEW sales" vs. "60% of sales"

Different secondary sources report this differently:

| Source | Exact Wording | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Techmeme (summarizing NYT) | "60% of its 2025 sales were non-US" | Total 2025 sales |
| InsiderFinance | "roughly 60% of new sales coming from outside the U.S., up from about 30% four years earlier" | NEW sales (acquisition), not total base |
| SaveDelete | "60% of sales now come from outside the US" | Total sales |
| Yahoo Finance/GuruFocus syndication | "About 60% of sales now come from outside the US across 60 countries" | Total sales |

**Critical ambiguity:** InsiderFinance's version says "60% of **new** sales" — this is a fundamentally different claim than "60% of total sales." If 60% of NEW member acquisitions are international, but the existing base is US-heavy, total international revenue could be much lower (e.g., 30-40%).

### Was This in the Official Press Release?

**No.** The WHOOP/BusinessWire Series G press release (whoop.com/press-center) does NOT mention any geographic revenue split. It says:
- "2.5 million members around the world"
- "ships to 56 countries, operates in six languages"
- "Proceeds will fuel further U.S. growth and international expansion"

The 60% figure appears to come from **WHOOP's communications with the NYT journalist** (likely background briefing or interview), not from the official press release. This is important: companies share favorable metrics with journalists that they don't put in official filings.

## 4. Analysis: "New Sales" vs. "Total Sales"

The InsiderFinance version ("60% of **new** sales") is the most plausible interpretation and resolves most contradictions:

### If 60% of NEW member acquisitions are international:
- Total active base could still be 55-65% US (legacy members from pre-2023 era)
- International REVENUE share could be 35-45% (lower ARPU in some intl markets, newer members = less accumulated revenue)
- This explains why "international expansion" is still a use of funds — the company is investing to maintain/grow the new-member mix
- This is consistent with a company that was "heavily US-weighted" historically but is rapidly shifting

### If 60% of TOTAL sales are international:
- The entire base has already flipped to majority-international
- ARPU analysis needs geographic segmentation (intl pricing may differ)
- The "international expansion" real-option is largely already exercised
- This would be a more dramatic claim and harder to reconcile with the contradicting evidence

### Implied Math Check

If 60% of new member adds are international:
- 2025 estimated gross adds: ~800K-1M (103% bookings growth implies rapid acquisition)
- International gross adds: ~480K-600K
- US gross adds: ~320K-400K
- Existing base (pre-2025): ~1.5M-1.7M (mostly US-acquired in earlier years)
- Result: total base could be ~55% US / ~45% international by end of 2025
- Revenue share might be ~50-55% US / ~45-50% international (if intl ARPU is slightly lower)

This is plausible and consistent with most evidence.

## 5. Verdict

| Assessment | Confidence |
|---|---|
| **"60% of new member acquisitions in 2025 were international"** | **HIGH (85%)** — credible NYT reporting, consistent with expansion trajectory, and the InsiderFinance version is most specific |
| **"60% of total 2025 sales/revenue were international"** | **MEDIUM (50%)** — possible if including hardware shipments and Techmeme's summary is accurate, but contradicted by expansion-framing and absence from official press |
| **"60% of total active member base is international"** | **LOW (30%)** — legacy US base is large; unlikely to have flipped this quickly |

### Recommended Model Treatment

**Do NOT use "60% international revenue" as a point estimate.** The claim is ambiguous between new sales vs. total sales, sourced from journalist interpretation of a management briefing, and absent from official filings.

**Instead, model as a range:**

| Scenario | International Revenue % | Total International Revenue ($M) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 30-35% | $195M-$228M (on $650M base) | Legacy US base dominates; intl ARPU lower |
| Base | 40-45% | $260M-$293M | 60% of new sales blended with US-heavy legacy base |
| Aggressive | 50-55% | $325M-$358M | Rapid base shift; intl pricing at parity |

### Real-Option Implication

**International expansion retains significant real-option value** even at 40-45% international revenue. The option is about:
1. Deepening penetration in existing 56 markets (most are shallow)
2. Entering new markets (GCC expansion just beginning)
3. Scaling B2B Unite internationally
4. Achieving parity ARPU in international markets

This is NOT a case where the option is "already exercised." International sales are growing but WHOOP's penetration in most non-US markets is minimal relative to the addressable population.
