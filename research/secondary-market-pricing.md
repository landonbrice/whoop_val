## 2. Common vs. Preferred Discount Analysis

### WHOOP-Specific Implied Discounts

Using the Series F preferred price ($4.76/share, from Pitchbook cap table) and secondary market common stock prices:

| Period | Preferred Ref Price | Common (Secondary) | Implied Discount | Notes |

| 2024 | $4.76 (Series F) | ~$1.34–$4.30 | **10–72%** | Wide range; improving through 2024 |
| Pre-Series G (early 2026) | $4.76 (Series F) | ~$1.70–$1.89 | **60–64%** | Still deeply discounted vs. last preferred price |
| Post-Series G (Apr 2026) | $11.23 (Forge) | $4.42–$6.88 (Hiive/NPM) | **39–61%** | Discount to Forge/algorithmic price; common still repricing |

### Interpreting the Discount Structure

The 2023 trough discount of 72-75% reflects three compounding factors:
1. **Lack of marketability (DLOM):** Illiquid private shares with no clear exit timeline
2. **Lack of control (DLOC):** Common stock has no liquidation preference, board seats, or information rights
3. **Company-specific risk premium:** During 2022-2024, WHOOP was in a "funding desert" with no new priced round to anchor value; SoftBank's portfolio was under stress

Post-Series G, the 39-61% discount between algorithmic prices and actual transaction prices reflects the gap between "what the company is worth" and "what you can sell common shares for today."

### Cap Table Context (from Pitchbook)

As of Series F filing:
- Total preferred shares: 479,928,865 (~63.4% of equity)
- Common/ESOP: ~36.6%
- All preferred has **1x non-participating pari passu** liquidation preferences
- Aggregate liquidation preference (through Series F): ~$291M (sum of original issue prices x shares)
- At a $10.1B exit, all preferred converts to common — the 1x non-participating structure means the waterfall is effectively a single class above ~$291M

**Series G dilution:** $575M / $10.1B = 5.69% dilution. Estimated Series G shares: ~51.2M new shares at ~$11.23/share.

## 6. Implications for WHOOP Valuation (DLOM, DLOC, Three-Stage Discount Framework)

### Recommended DLOM Range: 15–30%

| Factor | Assessment | Impact on DLOM |
|---|---|---|
| **Time to liquidity** | IPO likely 2027 (12-18 months) | Reduces DLOM (shorter horizon) |
| **Investor quality** | Sovereign wealth (QIA, Mubadala), strategic (Abbott, Mayo), crossover (IVP, Macquarie) | Reduces DLOM (credible exit path) |
| **Revenue scale** | $1.1B run-rate, cash flow positive | Reduces DLOM (de-risked fundamentals) |
| **Secondary market activity** | 27 live orders on Hiive; traded on Forge, NPM, EquityZen | Reduces DLOM (some liquidity exists) |
| **Oura tender offer comp** | 25% discount to last round | Anchors DLOM at ~25% for best-in-class comp |
| **EquityZen platform average** | 13-29% discount (2025 range) | Supports 15-30% range |
| **Market concentration** | WHOOP not in top 20 most-traded privates | Increases DLOM (thinner liquidity than mega-caps) |
| **Information asymmetry** | Limited financial disclosure; no public filings | Increases DLOM |

**Point estimate for modeling:** 20-25% DLOM (base case), consistent with:
- Oura tender offer at 25%
- EquityZen Q1-Q2 2025 averages (13-16%)
- NPM venture secondary average (22%)
- Adjustment upward for WHOOP's lower secondary market liquidity vs. top-20 privates

### Recommended DLOC Range: 5–15%

The discount for lack of control is modest because:
- All preferred is 1x non-participating pari passu (no punitive liquidation preference overhang)
- At IPO exit (>$291M aggregate preference), all preferred converts to common
- Common shareholders benefit proportionally from any value above the preference stack
- The Series G investor group (sovereign wealth, crossover) signals alignment toward IPO, not down-round protection

### Three-Stage Discount Framework Application

The project's three-stage framework ($10.1B Series G mark -> $11.2B IPO -> $13-15B public fair value) should incorporate these discounts as follows:

**Stage 1: Series G to IPO (current to ~2027)**
- Start: $10.1B (Series G post-money, preferred basis)
- DLOM applied to common: 20-25% -> common value ~$7.6-8.1B
- This is consistent with the Hiive/NPM secondary prices implying $4.9-6.2B, which represent an even deeper discount because they include selling pressure and thin liquidity

**Stage 2: IPO pricing**
- Typical IPO discount to first-day close: 10-20% (per academic literature)
- At IPO, DLOM goes to zero (shares become freely tradable after lockup)

**Stage 3: Public market fair value**
- No DLOM or DLOC applicable
- Value driven by public market comps and DCF

### Pre-Series G Secondary Prices as Sanity Check

The 2023 trough price of ~$1.18-$1.34/share (implied ~$1.1-1.2B valuation) vs. the Series F preferred valuation of $3.6B represents a 67-70% discount. This extreme discount reflects:
- Macro environment (2022-2023 tech downturn)
- No new priced round to re-anchor value
- SoftBank distress signaling (portfolio write-downs)
- Illiquidity premium in a dead market

The post-Series G compression to a 39-61% discount (Hiive/NPM vs. Forge) shows the re-rating is still propagating through secondary markets — common stock has not yet fully repriced to the new preferred benchmark.

2. **WHOOP 409A valuation history:** The 409A common stock FMV (used for employee option grants) would provide the most authoritative common-vs-preferred discount data. Not publicly available; would require insider disclosure or a 409A provider database.

3. **Oura per-share secondary pricing:** Notice.co shows $39.08/share but no historical trend. Forge and Hiive require login. Per-share data would enable a direct WHOOP-Oura DLOM comparison.

4. **Series G preferred terms:** The Series G may have different liquidation preferences (e.g., 1.5x, participating) given sovereign wealth participation. The 1x non-participating terms documented in Pitchbook are through Series F only.

5. **Post-Series G common stock repricing velocity:** How quickly do secondary market prices converge to the new preferred benchmark after a major round? Historical pattern data would inform the discount's time decay.
