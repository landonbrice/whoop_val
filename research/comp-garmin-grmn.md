## Company Overview

Garmin Ltd. is a Swiss-domiciled (operationally Olathe, KS) technology company that designs, manufactures, and markets GPS-enabled hardware and wearables across five reportable segments: Fitness, Outdoor, Aviation, Marine, and Auto OEM. Founded in 1989. Unlike WHOOP's pure subscription model, Garmin is a vertically integrated hardware manufacturer with minimal recurring software revenue. The relevant WHOOP comp slice is primarily the **Fitness segment**, which includes wearables, running watches, cycling computers, and health-tracking devices.

**Key distinction from WHOOP:** Garmin is a diversified hardware conglomerate. The Fitness segment (~33% of FY2025 revenue) is the closest analog to WHOOP's business, but Garmin has no subscription paywall -- hardware is the revenue model, with Garmin Connect as a free ecosystem layer.

## WHOOP Comparability Notes

### Why Garmin is in the comp set
Garmin is the **only publicly traded pure-play wearable company** at scale. It is the hardware-only anchor in Bucket 1. Apple Watch data is buried inside Apple's Wearables/Accessories segment (commingled with AirPods, HomePod) and cannot be isolated, making Garmin the sole viable public comp for consumer wearable hardware economics.

### Key comparability issues

1. **Revenue model mismatch.** Garmin sells hardware for upfront revenue with no subscription paywall. WHOOP's business is the inverse: subsidized/included hardware with recurring subscription revenue. This is the single largest comparability limitation.

2. **Diversification discount/premium.** Garmin's 6.7x EV/Revenue reflects a blended multiple across 5 segments with very different growth profiles and margin structures. The **Fitness segment alone** (33% growth, 31% operating margin, ~58% GM) would likely command a higher standalone multiple than the consolidated business, which is weighed down by the loss-making Auto OEM segment and slower-growth Aviation/Marine.

3. **Relevant comp is Fitness segment, not the whole company.** For WHOOP comping purposes, the Fitness segment is the correct reference:
   - FY2025 Fitness revenue: $2.36B (vs. WHOOP ~$1.1B run-rate)
   - Fitness growth: 33% YoY (vs. WHOOP 103%)
   - Fitness operating margin: 30.8% (WHOOP likely lower given earlier stage)
   - Fitness gross margin: ~58% (WHOOP subscription GM likely 70-80%)

4. **No subscription economics.** Garmin Connect is free. There are no churn, ARPU, LTV, or cohort retention metrics to compare against WHOOP. Garmin's "retention" is measured in hardware upgrade cycles (3-5 years), not monthly/annual subscriptions.

5. **Margin structure difference.** Garmin's 58% blended gross margin is exceptionally high for hardware but low for a subscription business. This reflects BOM costs, manufacturing, and distribution that WHOOP mostly avoids on the software side (though WHOOP does have hardware BOM costs on the device).

6. **Scale difference.** Garmin shipped 20M+ units in FY2025; WHOOP has 2.5M+ members. Garmin is a mature, profitable, dividend-paying company. WHOOP is pre-IPO growth stage.

### Implied WHOOP multiple range from Garmin

If Garmin Fitness standalone would trade at 8-10x revenue (premium to consolidated 6.7x due to growth and margin profile), and WHOOP grows 2-3x faster but has subscription rather than hardware economics, the hardware-comp leg of the football field suggests:

- **Bear (hardware-only view):** 3-5x revenue = $3.3B-$5.5B
- **Base (Fitness-comp adjusted):** 5-7x revenue = $5.5B-$7.7B
- **Bull (growth-premium applied):** 7-9x revenue = $7.7B-$9.9B

These are Bucket 1 only ranges; the overall valuation triangulates across all three buckets.
