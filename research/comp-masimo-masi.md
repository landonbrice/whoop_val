# Masimo (MASI) — Comparable Company Profile

**Bucket 3: Health Data / Medical Device**
**Role in comp set:** Sensor-relevant comp — pulse oximetry expertise, consumer health pivot with W1 watch
**Date compiled:** April 16, 2026

---

## Company Overview

- **Headquarters:** Irvine, CA
- **Founded:** 1989 by Joe Kiani (CEO until activist replacement)
- **Core business:** Noninvasive patient monitoring — pulse oximetry (Signal Extraction Technology / SET), brain monitoring, capnography, hemodynamics
- **Consumer pivot:** W1 medical watch (FDA 510(k) cleared for OTC and prescription SpO2/PR, 2023); consumer audio via Sound United acquisition (2022, subsequently divested 2025)
- **Sound United:** Acquired April 2022 for ~$1.025B (Bowers & Wilkins, Denon, Marantz, Polk Audio). Sold to HARMAN International (Samsung) in 2025 for $350M — a ~$675M value destruction
- **Pending M&A:** Danaher agreed to acquire Masimo for $180/share (~$9.9B EV) on Feb 17, 2026. ~38% premium to $130.15 pre-deal close. Expected to close H2 2026, shareholder vote May 1, 2026
- **Shares outstanding:** ~52.2M

### Consumer Health (W1 Watch)
- **W1 launch:** Limited market release 2022; full market release 2022; FDA 510(k) OTC/Rx clearance Nov 2023; telemonitoring connectivity clearance Aug 2024
- **W1 revenue:** Not separately disclosed; included in non-healthcare segment. FLAGGED — no public unit sales or revenue breakdown

### Apple Litigation
- Masimo won a $250M+ patent infringement verdict against Apple (pulse oximetry IP). Apple removed blood oxygen feature from Watch Series 9/Ultra 2 in the US. This IP portfolio is a significant asset that Danaher is acquiring.

## Sound United Acquisition Impact

### Timeline
- **Apr 2022:** Acquired Sound United for ~$1.025B
- **2024:** Announced plan to separate/spin off consumer businesses
- **May 2025:** Agreed to sell consumer audio to HARMAN/Samsung for $350M
- **2025:** Sale completed; non-healthcare moved to discontinued operations

### Financial Impact

| Metric | Pre-Acquisition (FY2021) | Post-Acquisition (FY2022-FY2024) |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $1,239M (100% healthcare) | $2,036-2,094M (healthcare ~65-67%) |

### Value Destruction
- Purchased for ~$1.025B (2022)
- Sold for $350M (2025)
- **Net value destruction: ~$675M** plus ~$304M goodwill impairment charge
- The acquisition distracted management, triggered a proxy fight (Politan Capital), and resulted in founder/CEO Joe Kiani's departure (2024)

### Organic Healthcare Growth vs. Consolidated
- Organic healthcare grew ~8-10% through the Sound United period
- Consolidated growth was flat-to-low-single-digits because non-healthcare revenue declined
- **For WHOOP comp purposes, healthcare-only metrics (post-divestiture) are the relevant comparison**

## WHOOP Comparability Notes

### Relevant Parallels
1. **Sensor technology DNA:** Masimo's 30+ years in noninvasive optical sensing (SpO2, perfusion index, hemoglobin) is the gold standard for clinical-grade wearable sensors. WHOOP's sensor stack is consumer-grade but aspires to clinical accuracy — Masimo represents the "what clinical-grade looks like" benchmark
2. **Consumer health pivot:** Both attempted to bring medical-grade sensing to consumer wearables. Masimo's W1 watch is the most direct medical-to-consumer crossover product in market
3. **FDA pathway:** Masimo's W1 has FDA 510(k) clearance for SpO2/PR — relevant precedent for WHOOP's own FDA-cleared ECG and blood pressure features
4. **Recurring revenue model:** Both rely on a hardware platform + ongoing revenue stream (Masimo: single-use sensors/consumables; WHOOP: subscription)
5. **Healthcare platform thesis:** Masimo's hospital installed base + SafetyNet telemonitoring parallels WHOOP's "health data platform" thesis, but from the clinical side in

### Key Differences
1. **Enterprise vs. consumer:** Masimo sells to hospitals (B2B/enterprise), WHOOP sells to consumers (D2C). Fundamentally different go-to-market, pricing power, and regulatory burden
2. **Hardware economics:** Masimo's razors-and-blades model (monitors + single-use sensors) is proven and high-margin (~63% healthcare GM). WHOOP's hardware + subscription model is unproven at scale
3. **Scale:** Masimo at ~$1.5B healthcare revenue dwarfs WHOOP at ~$1.1B run-rate. But Masimo took 30+ years; WHOOP got there in ~12 years
4. **The Sound United cautionary tale:** Masimo's disastrous consumer audio acquisition ($675M value destruction, CEO ousted, proxy fight) is a warning about medtech companies overreaching into consumer markets. The reverse risk exists for WHOOP — a consumer company reaching into healthcare
5. **M&A exit path:** Masimo being acquired by Danaher at ~6.5x revenue validates medtech multiples but represents a strategic acquisition premium. WHOOP's IPO path implies different valuation dynamics
6. **IP moat:** Masimo has foundational pulse oximetry patents (won $250M+ vs. Apple). WHOOP's IP moat is thinner — primarily algorithms and UX, not hardware IP

### Multiple Relevance for WHOOP
- **Healthcare-only EV/Revenue: ~6.4x** — this is the comp-relevant multiple
- **Danaher acquisition: ~6.5x revenue** — includes strategic/control premium, so represents ceiling
- The ~6-7x revenue range for Masimo healthcare supports the upper end of Bucket 3 (6-12x) but below the growth premium that iRhythm or Dexcom command
- Masimo's ~9% growth rate is meaningfully below WHOOP's 103% — WHOOP should trade at a significant growth premium if it can demonstrate sustainability
- **For Bucket 3 weighting purposes:** Masimo anchors the "proven medtech, moderate growth" end of the 6-12x range. Dexcom/iRhythm anchor the higher end
