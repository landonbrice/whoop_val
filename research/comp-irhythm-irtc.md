# iRhythm Technologies (IRTC) -- Comparable Company Profile

**Bucket:** B3 -- Health Data / Medical Device
**Role in comp set:** Cardiac wearable + SaaS -- closest parallel to WHOOP's ECG/cardiac monitoring ambitions
**Data as of:** April 16, 2026

---

## Company Overview

- **Founded:** 2006; IPO October 2016 (NASDAQ: IRTC)
- **Headquarters:** San Francisco, CA
- **CEO:** Quentin Blackford (since 2022)
- **Employees:** ~2,200 (est.)
- **Product:** Zio monitor -- FDA-cleared, single-use, adhesive ECG patch worn for up to 14 days, paired with ZEUS deep-learning algorithm for arrhythmia detection
- **Business model:** Integrated device + service (similar to WHOOP's hardware + subscription, but reimbursed by payers rather than consumer-paid)
- **Key products:**
  - **Zio XT:** Long-term continuous monitoring (up to 14 days), store-and-forward
  - **Zio AT:** Real-time wireless cardiac monitoring with cloud connectivity
  - **ZEUS:** FDA-cleared AI algorithm for ECG interpretation; classifies broad range of arrhythmias at cardiologist-level performance
- **Rebranded:** iRhythm Holdings, Inc. (effective January 2026)

## Reimbursement / Regulatory Landscape (CMS Rates, FDA Clearances)

### CMS Reimbursement History (Critical for WHOOP Healthcare Thesis)

| Period | Event | Rate / Impact |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Pre-2021 | Regional MAC pricing (National Government Services) | ~$311 per Zio report |
| Jan 2021 | Novitas Solutions (MAC) slashes rates | ~$103-$115 for CPT 93243/93247 |
| Q1 2021 | Stock drops 83% in 7 months; iRhythm discontinues Zio XT for Medicare segment | Existential reimbursement risk exposed |
| Jul 2022 | CMS proposes national pricing in 2023 PFS | Proposed ~$229; stock jumps 33% |
| Nov 2022 | CMS final rule for CY2023 | National rates: $213 (CPT 93243), $224 (CPT 93247) |
| 2023 | National rates effective; geographic adjustments | $216-$292 (93243), $227-$307 (93247) depending on GPCI location |
| 2024 | Updated supply item pricing | SD339 supply item: $260.35 |
| 2025 | Stable Medicare environment | Revenue growth accelerating, suggesting reimbursement headwinds resolved |

### CPT Code Reference
- **CPT 93243:** Extended ECG monitoring, >48 hours to 7 days (scanning analysis, interpretation, report)
- **CPT 93247:** Extended ECG monitoring, >7 days to 15 days (scanning analysis, interpretation, report)
- **SD339:** Supply item code for extended external ECG patch

### FDA Clearance History
- **Zio XT:** Original 510(k) clearance for long-term continuous ECG monitoring
- **Zio AT:** 510(k) cleared for real-time wireless cardiac monitoring; received additional 510(k) clearances in 2024 for design modifications
- **ZEUS system:** FDA-cleared AI/deep-learning algorithm for ECG analysis; clinically integrated with Zio AT for automated arrhythmia classification
- **2024 FDA warning letter:** FDA issued warning for design changes made without clearance and marketing for unauthorized patient population; iRhythm subsequently obtained 510(k) clearance for the changes

### Commercial Payer Coverage
- All major commercial insurance plans cover the Zio system
- Revenue mix is heavily commercial (est. 70-80%) with Medicare representing a smaller but growing share post-reimbursement resolution

*Sources: MedTech Dive (CMS national rate proposal, Jul 2022); MassDevice (CMS ruling commentary); MDDI Online (reimbursement analysis); iRhythm billing/reimbursement page; iRhythm investor relations (FDA clearance announcements)*

## WHOOP Comparability Notes

### Why iRhythm is in the Comp Set
1. **Cardiac wearable + AI/SaaS:** Both companies combine a wearable sensor with cloud-based analytics. iRhythm's Zio + ZEUS is structurally analogous to WHOOP's band + app/coach
2. **ECG as the bridge:** WHOOP received FDA clearance for ECG, entering iRhythm's core domain. This is the most direct overlap in the comp set
3. **Recurring revenue model:** iRhythm generates revenue per patient episode (effectively a per-use SaaS model); WHOOP generates subscription revenue. Both have high gross margins and negative operating margins trending toward profitability
4. **AI/algorithm moat:** Both companies position proprietary algorithms (ZEUS for iRhythm, WHOOP Coach for WHOOP) as key differentiation vs. hardware competitors

### Key Differences (Discount Factors)
1. **Reimbursement-driven vs. consumer-paid:** iRhythm's revenue depends on CMS/commercial payer reimbursement -- a regulatory risk WHOOP avoids but also a massive TAM advantage if WHOOP can access it
2. **Single-use vs. continuous wear:** Zio is a disposable patch for 14-day monitoring episodes prescribed by physicians; WHOOP is a continuous-wear consumer product. Different retention dynamics
3. **Clinical grade vs. wellness grade:** iRhythm's data feeds into clinical decision-making; WHOOP's data is self-directed wellness (though FDA ECG clearance narrows this gap)
4. **Channel:** iRhythm sells through hospitals/clinics (B2B2C); WHOOP sells direct-to-consumer (D2C)
5. **Margin structure:** Both have ~70% gross margins, but iRhythm's SG&A is 66%+ of revenue (field sales force + technician labor) vs. WHOOP's likely lower SG&A intensity as a D2C brand

### Reimbursement Pathway Relevance for WHOOP
iRhythm's reimbursement saga is the single most instructive case study for WHOOP's healthcare ambitions:

- **The opportunity:** If WHOOP can get CPT codes and payer coverage for its ECG or blood pressure monitoring, the revenue per user could step-change from ~$30/month subscription to $200-$300+ per monitoring episode
- **The risk:** iRhythm's stock dropped 83% when CMS cut reimbursement rates in 2021. Payer dependence creates existential regulatory risk
- **The timeline:** iRhythm spent years navigating CMS from regional MAC pricing to national rates. WHOOP is at the very beginning of this journey -- any reimbursement revenue is likely 3-5+ years away
- **The lesson:** Abbott and Mayo Clinic as WHOOP investors suggest the company is building clinical credibility, but FDA clearance alone does not guarantee reimbursement. The CPT code + CMS rate-setting process is separate and slower
- **Strategic implication for bucket weighting:** Assigning high weight to Bucket 3 (health data) for WHOOP implies confidence that WHOOP will navigate the reimbursement pathway that took iRhythm a decade+ to establish

### Implied Multiple Read-Through for WHOOP
- iRhythm trades at 5.6x LTM revenue / 4.8x NTM revenue with ~26% growth, ~70% gross margins, and approaching GAAP profitability
- At $1.1B run-rate revenue, applying iRhythm's 5.6x multiple would imply ~$6.2B EV for WHOOP
- The $10.1B Series G implies WHOOP is priced at ~9.2x revenue -- a significant premium to iRhythm, justified only if the market is pricing WHOOP's healthcare optionality + faster growth + larger TAM
- iRhythm's Rule of 40 score (~35) is similar to what WHOOP likely achieves (103% sub growth + cash flow positive), though WHOOP's growth rate is dramatically higher
