# Peloton Interactive (PTON) — 10-K Financial Analysis

**Purpose:** Extract detailed financial data from Peloton's SEC filings to build the bear-case comparable for WHOOP's valuation. Peloton is the single most important cautionary comp — same subsidized-hardware + subscription model, now trading at distressed multiples.

**Sources:** Peloton 10-K filings (FY2020–FY2025), quarterly shareholder letters, SEC EDGAR, StockAnalysis.com, Bullfincher.io, Backlinko, SGB Media, The CFO, Subscription Insider, CNBC, PeloBuddy, PYMNTS, Growthcurve.co.

**Peloton fiscal year ends June 30.** FY2024 = July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2024.

**Date compiled:** April 16, 2026

### Key Revenue Insight — The Mix Shift

The most critical trend for the WHOOP comp analysis: Peloton's revenue mix inverted from ~80% hardware / ~20% subscription (FY2019-FY2020) to ~33% hardware / ~67% subscription (FY2025). This mirrors WHOOP's own trajectory but Peloton's shift was driven by **collapsing hardware demand**, not growing subscription. WHOOP's subscription growth is organic and accretive.

This is the single most important metric deterioration in Peloton's story.

## 4. Unit Economics (Derived)

### Subscriber Acquisition Cost (Derived)

Using SG&A as proxy for S&M (SG&A includes S&M, not broken out separately in the income statement):

| Fiscal Year | SG&A ($M) | Net CF Sub Adds | Implied SAC (SG&A / Net Adds) |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY2020 | ~$550 (est.) | +580K | ~$948 |
| FY2021 | $1,390 | +1,239K | ~$1,122 |
| FY2022 | $1,982 | +636K | ~$3,116 |
| FY2023 | $1,446 | +64K | ~$22,594 |
| FY2024 | $1,310 | -49K (net loss) | N/M (negative) |
| FY2025 | $949 | Declining | N/M (negative) |

**Critical Insight:** SAC exploded from ~$1,000 in FY2020-FY2021 to effectively infinite in FY2023+ as Peloton is losing net subscribers despite continued S&M spend. Even in the "good" pandemic years, Peloton spent ~$1,000+ to acquire each Connected Fitness subscriber. When growth stalled, the model broke.

### LTV/CAC Framework (Illustrative, using FY2024 economics)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly subscription ARPU (CF only) | ~$48 |
| Subscription gross margin | 67.8% |
| Monthly gross profit per sub | ~$32.50 |
| Monthly churn | 1.9% |
| Expected lifetime (1/churn) | 52.6 months (~4.4 years) |
| Gross LTV per subscriber | ~$1,710 |
| SAC (FY2021 benchmark) | ~$1,100 |
| LTV/CAC | ~1.6x |

At pandemic-era economics (0.62% churn): LTV ~ $5,240, LTV/CAC ~ 4.8x.
At current economics with no net adds: LTV/CAC is meaningless — the company cannot acquire net new subscribers profitably.

**Growthcurve.co estimated (FY2025 illustrative):** LTV $1,540, CAC $130 (app-only, very different from hardware subscribers), CAC payback 8.4 months. This is for digital-only subscribers, which have very different economics.

## 8. WHOOP Implications — Bear Case Construction

### Why Peloton Matters for WHOOP's Valuation

Peloton is the closest public-market analog to WHOOP's business model: subsidized/break-even hardware driving a high-margin subscription. The bull thesis for WHOOP must specifically address why WHOOP avoids Peloton's fate.

### Key Parallels

| Dimension | Peloton | WHOOP |
|---|---|---|
| Model | Hardware + subscription | Hardware + subscription |
| Hardware economics | Sold at/below cost | Given "free" with subscription |
| Subscription pricing | $44/month | $30/month (annual) |
| Retention mechanism | Large sunk cost (bike) | Wearable lock-in + data history |
| Category | At-home fitness | Health/fitness wearable |
| Peak hype | 2020-2021 (COVID) | 2025-2026 (health data) |
| Key risk | Gym reopenings killed demand | Consumer wearable fatigue? |

### Critical Peloton Warnings for WHOOP Bear Case

1. **S&M addiction is fatal.** Peloton spent 35-55% of revenue on SG&A during growth years, then couldn't acquire subscribers profitably when growth slowed. At scale, the marginal subscriber was unprofitable. WHOOP must demonstrate efficient subscriber acquisition at 2.5M+ members.

2. **Churn is the silent killer.** Peloton's churn rose from 0.31% to 1.9% — a 6x increase — as the novelty wore off and competition intensified. If WHOOP's churn deteriorates similarly from a low base, subscription revenue plateaus and then declines despite marketing spend.

3. **Hardware commoditization.** Peloton's connected fitness products went from positive gross margin to deeply negative as competitors (NordicTrack, Echelon, free gym content) eroded pricing power. WHOOP faces the same threat from Apple Watch, Garmin, Oura, and Samsung.

4. **Growth-to-decline can be sudden.** Peloton went from +120% revenue growth (FY2021) to -11% (FY2022) in a single year. WHOOP's 103% subscription growth could decelerate sharply if the addressable market saturates faster than expected.

5. **The "platform" narrative can collapse.** Peloton was valued as a "connected fitness platform" at $50B+ market cap (Jan 2021). Today it trades at $1.5B — a 97% destruction of value. The market rapidly re-rates from "platform" to "hardware company" when growth stalls.

### What WHOOP Must Prove to Avoid Peloton's Fate

1. **Structural retention advantage.** WHOOP's 24/7 passive wearable creates deeper habit than Peloton's active-use model. If WHOOP can demonstrate >90% 12-month retention at scale (not just early adopters), this is the key differentiator.

2. **Healthcare optionality is real.** Peloton had no healthcare pivot. WHOOP's FDA clearances, Abbott/Mayo partnerships, and clinical-grade data give it a second revenue pillar that Peloton never had. But optionality must convert to revenue.

3. **Lower CAC at scale.** WHOOP's viral/word-of-mouth acquisition vs. Peloton's TV-ad-driven model. If WHOOP's SAC stays below $200-300 while Peloton's was $1,000+, the unit economics are fundamentally different.

4. **Hardware cost discipline.** WHOOP's small wearable BOM is likely $50-100, vs. Peloton's $1,500+ bike. Hardware losses per subscriber are an order of magnitude smaller.

### Bear Case Valuation Implications

If WHOOP follows a Peloton-like trajectory (growth stalls, churn rises, market re-rates):
- **Peloton's current EV/Revenue: ~1.0x** on $2.5B revenue
- **Applied to WHOOP's $1.1B revenue run-rate: $1.1B–1.5B** enterprise value
- **Applied to WHOOP at Peloton's peak multiple (~10x trailing): $11B** (roughly the Series G)
- **Peloton's trough multiple (~0.5x forward): $0.5B–0.8B** for WHOOP

The bear case for WHOOP is a valuation range of **$1.5B–3.0B** using Peloton's post-peak multiples on WHOOP's current revenue, representing a **70-85% haircut** from the $10.1B Series G mark.

### Base Case Differentiation

The base case argues WHOOP is structurally different because:
- 24/7 passive monitoring creates higher switching costs than active-use equipment
- Healthcare/clinical applications provide a TAM expansion Peloton never had
- Lower hardware cost = less balance sheet risk
- Smaller addressable market but higher willingness-to-pay cohort

This supports a multiple of 4-7x revenue ($4.4B–$7.7B), acknowledging Peloton risk but crediting the structural differences.
