## Company Overview

DexCom, Inc. is a San Diego-based medical device company that designs, manufactures, and commercializes continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems for people with diabetes and, increasingly, general metabolic health consumers. Founded in 1999. Dexcom's core product portfolio includes the G7 CGM (prescription, 10-day and 15-day wear), Stelo (OTC, launched August 2024 for non-insulin-using Type 2 and general wellness), and related software/data platforms. Revenue is ~91% disposable sensors (recurring) with the remainder from transmitters and receivers. Dexcom holds an estimated 74% US CGM market share (2024) and serves 2.8M+ customers worldwide.

**Key distinction from WHOOP:** Dexcom is a pure-play FDA-regulated medical device company with reimbursement-driven revenue (insurance/payer coverage). WHOOP is consumer-direct subscription without reimbursement. Dexcom's Stelo OTC launch is the bridge -- it moves Dexcom toward WHOOP's consumer model, while WHOOP's FDA clearances (ECG, blood pressure) move WHOOP toward Dexcom's clinical model. Dexcom trades at health-data/medical-device multiples that represent the aspirational ceiling for WHOOP if its healthcare pivot succeeds.

| Metric | FY2021 | FY2022 | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **R&D ($M)** | $604 | $484 | $506 | $552 | $599 |
| **R&D % Revenue** | 24.7% | 16.6% | 14.0% | 13.7% | 12.9% |
| **SG&A ($M)** | $811 | $1,008 | $1,185 | $1,286 | $1,291 |
| **SG&A % Revenue** | 33.1% | 34.6% | 32.7% | 31.9% | 27.7% |
| **Total OpEx ($M)** | $1,415 | $1,492 | $1,691 | $1,838 | $1,890 |
| **Total OpEx % Revenue** | 57.8% | 51.3% | 46.7% | 45.6% | 40.5% |

Source: [StockAnalysis.com DXCM Financials](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/dxcm/financials/).

**Notable:** R&D as % of revenue has declined sharply from ~25% to ~13%, reflecting operating leverage as the G7 platform matures. SG&A has also declined from ~33% to ~28% as the sales force scales. Total opex as % of revenue compressed from 58% to 41% over five years -- significant operating leverage. Dexcom does not break SG&A into selling/marketing vs. G&A in its public filings; it is reported as a single combined line item.

**FY2021 R&D spike note:** The $604M R&D in FY2021 (vs. $484M in FY2022) reflects elevated investment during the G7 development cycle. The decline to ~$484M in FY2022 was partly due to G7 development completion and partly capitalization changes. R&D has since resumed growth in absolute dollars but continues to decline as % of revenue.

## Historical Valuation Context

Dexcom's stock and multiples have been volatile:

- **Pre-2024 peak:** Stock traded above $160 in late 2023/early 2024, implying EV/Revenue of ~12--14x and market cap of ~$60B+
- **July 2024 guidance cut:** Stock dropped ~40% in a single session after Dexcom cut FY2024 revenue guidance. This compressed EV/Revenue from ~10x to ~6x
- **Current (April 2026):** Stock at $61.54, EV/Revenue ~5x -- the lowest the stock has traded on a multiple basis since the pre-COVID era
- **Implication for WHOOP:** Dexcom's multiple compression from ~12x to ~5x illustrates the risk in health-data/medical-device multiples. Even with 16% revenue growth and 23% FCF margins, the market repriced Dexcom significantly lower. WHOOP's aspirational Bucket 3 ceiling should reference the ~5x current multiple, not the ~12x peak.

## WHOOP Comparability Notes

1. **Strongest health-data comp in the set.** Dexcom's business model -- FDA-regulated sensor hardware + recurring consumable revenue (~91% sensors) -- is the closest public analog to where WHOOP aspires to go with its healthcare pivot. The sensor replacement cycle (10--15 days) creates subscription-like recurring revenue, analogous to WHOOP's monthly subscription.

2. **The multiple compression story is the cautionary tale.** Dexcom traded at 10--14x EV/Revenue when the growth narrative was intact, then collapsed to ~5x after a single guidance miss. This demonstrates that health-data/medical-device premiums are fragile and growth-dependent. For WHOOP's Bucket 3 weighting, the current ~5x (not the historical ~12x) is the more appropriate anchor.

3. **Stelo = the convergence play.** Dexcom's OTC Stelo launch (consumer wellness, no prescription) and WHOOP's FDA clearances (ECG, blood pressure) represent the two companies converging from opposite directions. Dexcom moving into consumer wellness validates WHOOP's thesis that wearable health data has mainstream consumer demand beyond diagnosed medical conditions. However, Stelo also represents a competitive threat if CGM-for-wellness gains traction in WHOOP's target demographic.

4. **Operating leverage trajectory is instructive.** Dexcom's opex compression from 58% to 41% of revenue over five years, with FCF margin expanding from 2% to 23%, provides a template for what WHOOP's long-term margin profile could look like if it achieves scale. However, Dexcom had the advantage of insurance reimbursement (higher willingness-to-pay) which WHOOP currently lacks.

5. **Implied WHOOP Bucket 3 range: 5--8x revenue.** Using Dexcom's current 5x EV/Revenue as the floor (reflecting post-correction reality) and a modest growth premium for WHOOP's faster growth rate (103% vs. 16%), a reasonable Bucket 3 range for WHOOP is 5--8x. The 12x historical peak should not be used without explicit justification.
